The Sanchez Center for Health Equity

Founded to address the disparities in live kidney transplants for Black and Brown communities, The Sanchez Center for Health Equity is a beacon of hope and support. We focus on increasing awareness, providing direct support, and developing platforms and campaigns to improve match rates for these communities.

Our Initiatives

  • Data-driven awareness and multi-modal narration of racial disparities.
  • Development of a high engagement matching platform.
  • National campaign focusing on the health of Black and Brown communities.

Our Center

The Jentosy Project, a part of our center, is dedicated to a national campaign that addresses the significant disparities faced by Black and Brown communities in organ transplantation. With a history of systemic biases and trust issues, our goal is to bridge gaps, educate, and provide equitable healthcare opportunities.

Background

Our founder's personal journey through dialysis and transplant processes highlighted the severe inequities in the healthcare system, especially for people of color. This experience, combined with a strong academic background and personal challenges, including overcoming injustice, shaped the mission of our center.

Our Mission

Our mission is to ensure that Black and Brown individuals receive equitable healthcare, particularly in the realm of kidney transplants. We strive to reduce the waiting time for these communities, assist in finding live donors, and provide necessary support throughout the transplantation process.
